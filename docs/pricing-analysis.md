# TarotByte — Pricing Sanity Check (Feb 2025 market data)

## Short answer
Your instincts are right. **$2 member / $4 list per Decan Engine add-on, with 1 free/month, is well within the market — and if anything it sits on the affordable-but-not-cheap end, which is exactly where a credible-but-accessible brand wants to be.** You are NOT undercutting the market in a way that signals "low quality." You're undercutting the *live human* market (which you should — you're automated), while pricing your subscription right in the sweet spot of the *app* market.

---

## What the market actually charges (verified, 2025–2026)

### Live human readings (per-minute — the expensive tier)
These are your *ceiling*, not your competition. You're automated, so you can and should be far below them.
| Platform | Rate |
|---|---|
| Purple Garden | from **$0.99/min** (intro $30 credit) |
| Keen | **$1.99/min** regular (5 min for $1 intro) |
| Psychic Source | **$4.99/min** regular |
| PathForward | **$5/min** flat |
| California Psychics | **$1 / $2 / $4/min** tiers |
| Nebula (human psychic) | **~$3.99/min** average |
- In-person / hour-long human readings: **$40–$100/hr**; online **$50–$80/hr**.
- **Takeaway:** a 15-minute human reading = ~$30–$60. A single "reading" from a human costs many multiples of your whole month.

### App / automated subscriptions (your ACTUAL competitive set)
| Platform | Price |
|---|---|
| Nebula | **$7.99/week**, **$39.99/3-month** (~$13.33/mo), **$49.99/year** (~$4.17/mo) |
| Astrology Zone, Trusted Tarot, Co–Star, The Pattern | free + IAP, typically **$4.99–$9.99/mo** premium tiers |
- Monthly-spread subscription boxes (e.g. Homebound "Tarot Dispatch"): a few $ per month for a mailed spread.
- **Takeaway:** the automated/app subscription sweet spot is **~$5–$10/month**, or **~$40–$50/year**.

---

## Where TarotByte should sit

### Per-reading Decan Engine add-on
- **Your $2 member / $4 list is good.** For context, $4 buys ~1 minute with a mid-tier human. You're offering a *complete, named, decan-precise reading* for that — strong perceived value.
- Keep the **1 free/month** for members — it's a great retention hook and costs you ~nothing (it's automated).
- Optional tweak: consider a **3-pack** ("3 Decan readings for $5") to lift average order value without raising the single price.

### Subscription (when you launch it — Phase 2)
Recommended structure that matches the market:
| Tier | Suggested price | What they get |
|---|---|---|
| **Free** | $0 | Yes/No + 3-card, Classic layer, The Weekly Byte |
| **Member** (email signup) | $0 | + Celestial layer, Energy Reading, **1 free Decan add-on/mo**, $2 add-on price |
| **Subscriber** | **$5.99–$7.99/mo** or **$49/yr** | **Unlimited Decan Engine**, all paid spreads (Celtic Cross, Horseshoe, 5-Card V), saved reading history, astrology overlays |
- **$49/year** puts you dead-level with Nebula's annual and reads as "serious product, fair price."
- Annual should be your hero price (best margin, best retention). Show monthly to anchor it as the expensive option.

### Positioning line you can use
> "A single reading from a live psychic can cost $30–$60. TarotByte gives you unlimited, decan-precise readings for less than the price of one coffee a month."

---

## The one caution
Don't go *below* ~$4.99/mo on the subscription. Under that, you start signaling "free-tier gimmick" rather than "credible tool," and you leave money on the table you'll want for API + hosting + domain costs. $2/$4 add-on + $49/yr subscription is a coherent, defensible ladder.
